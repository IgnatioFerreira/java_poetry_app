							<p>The wonderful workings of the world: wonderful,<br />
wonderful: I'm surprised half the time:<br />
ground up fine, I puff if a pebble stirs:<br />
<br />
I'm nervous: my moarality's intricate: if<br />
a squash blossom dies, I feel withered as a stained<br />
zucchini and blame my nature: and<br />
when grassblades flop to the little red-ant<br />
queens burring around trying to get aloft, I blame<br />
my not keeping the grass short, stubble<br />
<br />
firm: well, I learn a lot of useless stuff, meant<br />
to be ignored: like when the sun sinking in the<br />
west glares a plane invisible, I think how much<br />
<br />
revelation concealment necessitates: and then I<br />
think of the oecean, multiple to a blinding<br />
oneness and realize that only total expression<br />
expressed hiding: I'll have to say everything<br />
to take on the roundness and withdrawal of the deep dark:<br />
less than total is a bucketful of radiant toys.</p>
